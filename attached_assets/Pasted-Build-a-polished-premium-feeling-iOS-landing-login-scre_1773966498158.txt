Build a polished, premium-feeling iOS landing / login screen for an app called QuotePro.

Goal:
Make this screen feel like a 10/10 App Store-quality SaaS product for residential cleaning businesses. The screen should increase trust, improve conversion, and make users want to tap immediately. Keep the overall dark, modern, premium feel, but improve hierarchy, spacing, conversion psychology, and iOS-native polish.

Platform:
iOS-first design. Use SwiftUI-style visual patterns even if building in React Native or another framework. It must feel like a real iPhone app, not a web page inside an app.

Brand:
App name: QuotePro
Audience: residential cleaning business owners and teams
Core value proposition: create professional quotes fast, raise average ticket size, and close more jobs
Tone: premium, trustworthy, fast, modern, clean, revenue-focused

Current strengths to preserve:
- Dark mode premium aesthetic
- Strong headline around speed and closing more jobs
- Good / Better / Best pricing preview
- Clear primary CTA
- Simple, uncluttered layout

What to improve:
1. Add stronger conversion-focused hierarchy
2. Add trust and credibility near the top
3. Make the sample quote section feel more interactive and useful
4. Improve CTA wording and clarity
5. Make sign in feel secondary but still polished
6. Add subtle emotional positioning for cleaning business owners
7. Make spacing, typography, and button styling feel truly native to iOS
8. Keep everything visually balanced on modern iPhone screens

Screen content structure:
1. Top area
- Keep language selector, but make it feel cleaner and more intentional
- Add proper top safe-area spacing
- Show QuotePro logo and app name prominently
- Add a short, high-converting headline:
  “Close More Cleaning Jobs in 30 Seconds”
- Add a supporting subheadline:
  “Create professional Good / Better / Best quotes that help customers say yes faster.”
- Add a small trust row under the subheadline with 2 to 3 short proof points, for example:
  “Built for cleaning companies”
  “Professional customer-facing quotes”
  “Increase average ticket size”

2. Sample quote card
- Turn the sample section into a premium card or module, not just loose text
- Add a label like “See what your customer sees”
- Display a home profile selector above the price options, such as:
  “3 bed / 2 bath home”
- Show three pricing tiers:
  Good — $180
  Better — $240
  Best — $320
- Make the “Better” option visually selected by default
- Add subtle explanatory text:
  “Customers are more likely to choose mid-tier and premium options when presented clearly.”
- Make the card feel interactive, even if only visually
- Add soft glow, depth, or subtle iOS-native emphasis, but do not overdesign it

3. Primary CTA
- Replace “Create Your First Quote” with a higher-converting option:
  “Create Free Quote”
- Add a small subtext underneath:
  “No complicated setup”
- Make the button full-width, large, premium, and highly tappable
- Use iOS-style corner radius and shadow treatment
- It should feel like the obvious next step

4. Secondary CTA
- Keep “Sign In to Your Account” as the secondary option
- Make it clearly secondary but still elegant
- Use an outlined or low-emphasis filled style
- Add strong spacing between primary and secondary actions

5. Bottom reassurance area
- Add a short reassurance line near the bottom:
  “Save quotes, send them to customers, and close jobs faster.”
- Optionally add a tiny testimonial or result snippet:
  “Cleaning companies use QuotePro to present higher-value options with confidence.”

Visual direction:
- Premium dark mode only
- Clean black or near-black background
- Bright electric blue accents
- High contrast, crisp typography
- Native iOS spacing and proportions
- Rounded cards and buttons
- Avoid clutter, avoid feeling like a dashboard
- Use subtle motion-ready design cues if appropriate
- The final result should feel like a mix of Apple-native polish and modern SaaS conversion design

Typography:
- Bold, clean headline
- Clear information hierarchy
- Supporting text should be readable and not too small
- Price typography should be strong and immediately scannable

UX requirements:
- Respect iPhone safe areas
- Make primary CTA visible without scrolling on most modern iPhones
- Optimize for thumb-friendly interaction
- Ensure the screen looks balanced on smaller and larger iPhones
- Support dark mode beautifully
- Keep the experience focused on one core action: start quoting

Conversion goals:
The user should immediately understand:
- what QuotePro does
- who it is for
- why it matters
- why they should trust it
- what they should tap next

Deliverable:
Create the full screen UI with production-quality styling and component structure.
Use reusable components for:
- language selector
- hero section
- trust badges
- sample quote card
- pricing tier buttons
- primary and secondary CTA buttons
- reassurance footer

Important:
Do not make it look generic, templated, or like a website.
Make it feel like a premium iOS app that could rank well in the App Store and convert cleaning business owners into users immediately.
